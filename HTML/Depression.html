What is Deprerssion?

Depression is a severe medical condition that affects your ability to function normally. It is considered as a mood disorders in the medical field and it carries a number of symptoms that affect people in their ocupation, education, social and mental life. Many who have depression suffer the inability to remember a happy or prideful past as they would be riddled with bad experinces and thoughts. During a depressive episode, a person with depression often experience different thoughts than before they had a depressive episode. Thus, it makes an already diffuclt condition to be diagnosed much harder to diagnose.

Risk Factors

Here are the risk factors that may increase the likelihood that you or your loved one may develop depression:

  • Genetics Traits & Family History.

    • An academic journal has proven that if one of your close family member has developed depression, the chances of you developing depression may be as high as 40% to 50%. This almost guarantee that you will develop this condition.

  • A long history of trauma and abuse.

    • Those with a history of unresolved trauma will have lingering negative thoughts. This thoughts if not treated will cause many negative emotional issues; namely depression, a study finds.

  • Grief or loss that is still unresolved.
  • Had a history of using drugs or is currently using drugs.
  • Gender
  • Chronicly Stressed

Deprerssion Symptoms

Depression can affect an entire's person mental and physical health.

Physical Symptoms:

  • Easily Frustrated.
  • Consistenly sad or have an "empty" mood
  • Changes in sleep patterns.
  • Changes in dietary patterns.
  • Substance Abuse

Mental Symptoms:

  • Feeling of hopelessness.
  • Lack of enjoyment in intrests and hobbies.
  • Thoughts of sucides; attempts / plans
  • Diffculty concentrating.

Treatments for Deprerssion

Whilst depression is a serious issue, it can also be treated. The treatement for depression includes:

  • Counselling

    • You would be able to gain more insight on where the problem actually lies at. Your counselor will also be able to help you develop coping skills.

  • Medications
  • Self-Help

    • Praticing a healthy diet, sleep pattern as well as getting regular execrise can improve depression symptoms. Spending time with the people you love and care about is also likely to brighten up your mood, which will ease symptoms


Preventions for Depression

Depresion can be prevented by recieving enough sleep and execrise. Moreover, applying self-care methods such as looking after oneself will also likely boost a person's mental health. Additionally, having a close friend or a loved one that is understanding, happy to spend time doing hobbies that you both enjoy will prevent the feeling of loneliness or depression from occuring.
